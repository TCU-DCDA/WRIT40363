üéØ Course Description

"Explores the complex relationship between new media, culture, and design, with particular emphasis on the production of mobile applications and eBooks publications."

This advanced course focuses on multimedia authoring principles to develop mobile applications and electronic books that support scholarly work in the humanities and social sciences. Students will master HTML, CSS, and JavaScript while learning to create interactive digital experiences.

This is an introductory course designed for students interested in digital media and working effectively in digital environments.

üéì Learning Objectives

Primary Objectives

  1. Students will use their skill in web development as an exploratory research tool designed to address important cultural questions.
  2. Students will reflect on the limitations of multimodal communication common to multimedia authoring.

Program Standards

  1. Students will demonstrate an ability to compose digital arguments [DCDA0]
  2. Students will analyze how rhetorical acts and texts emerge from specific cultural discourses [WRIT02]
  3. Students will use writing to gain and express understanding of discipline-specific content [TCU Core]

üßë‚Äçüè´ Teaching Methods

The first third of our semester focuses on mastering the fundamental skills of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript through a series of hands-on lab exercises. The remainder of our time will be devoted to applying these digital authoring skills to web applications relevant to scholarly work in the Humanities and Social Sciences.

Our approach combines theoretical discussion, hands-on demonstrations, guided practice, and individual mentorship.

This course emphasizes meaningful feedback and self-reflection over traditional point accumulation. Throughout the semester, you will receive detailed guidance marked as Exceeds, Meets, or Fails to Meet Expectations.
